about us

Patoma is a New York City real estate investment, development, and management firm founded in early 2012. The firm focuses on value-add investments in emerging neighborhoods with strong access to public transportation. To date, these investments have centered on the Brooklyn neighborhoods of Bushwick And Williamsburg and, specifically, their residential and retail sectors. Patoma is led by Paul Henry and Tom Finnican.

leadership team

PAUL HENRY

Prior to co–founding Henry Development and Patoma, Paul worked at McKinsey and Company, the global management consulting firm, where he was involved in a variety of projects in the consumer product, financial services and telecom industries.

While working at McKinsey and living in New York City, Paul saw the need for affordable housing for young people. He and nearl yall of his friends lived in “converted” apartments where one or more bedrooms had been added by the tenants. In early 2006, Paul decided to capitalize on this lack of affordable, share apartments by forming Henry Development LLC and building 2–, 3–, and 4–BR apartments in Brooklyn.

Paul graduated summa cum laude from the School of Engineering and Applied Sciences at the University of Pennsylvania in 2004 where he received a B.S. in Systems Engineering and a minor in Mathematics

TOM FINNICAN

Prior to co-founding Patoma, Tom worked for a number of New York City real estate operators investing in, developing and managing residential units in Manhattan, Brooklyn, and the Bronx. His responsibilities included acting as owner’s rep for the development of a 47-unit gut renovation/new construction project as well as a 32-unit ground up development, in addition to leading the acquisition, financings and asset management of over $50 million of additional Brooklyn residential and commercial assets.

In 2007, Tom joined Paul at the Henry Development, bringing his development and constructions experience to the growing firm.

Tom graduated from the School of Arts and Sciences at the University of Pennsylvania in 2004 where he received a B.S. in Economics and Sociology.
